About me

I am currently a PhD candidate at the University of Edinburgh and visiting PhD student at Technical University of Munich under the supervision of Prof. Pramod Bhatotia. My research interest lies in the field of computer system design aiming mainly to build secure and reliable systems incorporating the now-emerging Persistent Memory Technology and leveraging modern Trusted Hardware Modules such as Intel SGX.

Before joining University of Edinburgh, I was working at AKKA DNO GmbH, as a Software Engineer Consultant. My main responsibilities in this position were related to the development of HMI Applications and Embedded C code development/analysis in the automotive sector.

Regarding my educational background, I completed my studies in National Technical University of Athens holding a 5 years’ degree followed by a 6 months‘ diploma thesis (Master in Engineering, MEng). My diploma thesis was about algorithms for task-applications offloading in a two-tiered cloud computing environment. During my studies I have gained a very strong background in computer science and software engineering which helped me to successfully complete numerous academic projects. I also gained experience as a Backend Database Developer at Travelosoft Ltd., a company which produces software related to travel management.
